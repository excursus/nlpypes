Navigation Guide

One of the features exclusive to Penguin Enhanced e-Book Classics is links that take the reader from the text to end notes and illustrations. Below you’ll find instructions on how to use these links based on the file format you’re using. Due to the number of portable electronic devices available, we are unable to provide specific instructions for every device/software scenario. If you should have further questions please contact the device or software manufacturer.

Adobe Reader

• To select an end note, click on the numbered link on the screen.

• To return to where you were in the story, press Alt+Left Arrow on a PC or Option+Left Arrow on a Mac.

Adobe Digital Editions

• Before you link to an end note, you’ll need to create a bookmark so that you can return to where you were in the story. To do so click on the bookmark icon at the top of your window. You will have the option to enter a descriptive comment, but it is not required.

• To select an end note, click on the numbered link on the screen.

• To return to where you were in the story, click on the bookmark you created.

Note: The order of the bookmarks corresponds to where they appear in the text. However if you do not attach descriptive comments, it may become difficult to distinguish between them.

Amazon Kindle

• To access an end note, use the select wheel to highlight the line that contains the numbered link then push the select wheel. You will see a new screen that offers an option to view the end note. Push the select wheel again to view the note.

• To returnto where you were in the story, press the Back button.

eReader

• To select an end note, click on the numbered link on the screen.

• The eReader platform displays notes and illustrations in two ways:

1. Hyperlinks. Notes from the Penguin Classics print edition and illustrations exclusive to the e-book edition are displayed through hyperlinks. Clicking on the numbered link will take you to another area of the text. To return to where you were in the story, click on the back arrow icon in the lower left hand corner of your screen.

2. Pop-up Windows. Notes exclusive to the e-book edition are displayed in pop-up windows. Clicking on the numbered creates a new window on top of the text you are reading. Click OK to return to where you were in the story.

Microsoft Reader

• To select an end note, click on the numbered link on the screen.

• The Microsoft Reader platform displays notes and illustrations in two ways:

1. Hyperlinks. Notes from the Penguin Classics print edition and illustrations exclusive to the e-book edition are displayed through hyperlinks. Clicking on the numbered link will take you to another area of the text. To return to where you were in the story, press Alt+R.

2. Pop-up Windows. Notes exclusive to the e-book edition are displayed in pop-up windows. Clicking on the numbered link creates a new window on top of the text you are reading. To return to where you were in the story, press Alt+R.

Mobipocket

• To select an end note, click on the numbered link on the screen.

• To go back to where you were in the story, click on the back arrow icon in the upper left of the screen.

Sony Reader

• To select a note, highlight it using the arrow controls on the lower right button. Push the button at the center of the arrow controls to view the note.

• To go back to where you were in the story, push the left arrow button.

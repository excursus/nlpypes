Filmography of Herman Melville’s Moby-Dick (1851)

I. Full-Length Productions of Moby-Dick

The Sea Beast (1926): directed by Millard Webb, written by Bess Meredyth, and starring John Barrymore as Ahab.

Moby Dick (1930): directed by Lloyd Bacon, screenplay by Oliver H. P. Garrett, and again starring John Barrymore as Ahab.

Moby Dick (1956): directed by John Huston, screenplay by Ray Bradbury, and starring Gregory Peck as Ahab.

Moby Dick (1971): directed and written by Orson Welles; shot in 1971 but not edited until 1999, when Filmmuseum München edited the rushes according to late Welles’s play Moby Dick—Rehearsed.

Moby Dick (1978): directed by Paul Stanley, and starring Jack Aranson as Ahab, Starbuck, Ishmael, and Father Mapple.

Moby Dick (1998): directed by Franc Roddam, teleplay by Anton Diether, and starring Patrick Stewart as Ahab.

II. Movies That Play on the Title Moby-Dick

Moby Dick Rehearsed (1955): made for television version of the Orson Welles play.

Moby Duck (1965): Daffy Duck cartoon.

Moby Dick and the Mighty Mightor (1967): Hanna-Barbera animated television series.

Petimata ot “Mobi Dik” (1970): Bulgarian film known in English as The Five from the Moby Dick.

Mlad muz a bílá velryba (1978): Czech film known in English as The Young Man and Moby Dick.

Thar She Blows: The Making of “Moby Dick” (1998): made for television (about the making of the 1998 television version).

Animated Epics: Moby Dick (2000): cartoon made for television.

Moby Dick: The True Story (2002): made for television.

Moby Dick et le secret de Mu (2005): Luxembourgian/French animated television series released in the UK as Moby Dick and the Secret of Mu.
